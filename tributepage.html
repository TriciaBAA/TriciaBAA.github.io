Some of Schenck's Most Famous Works:

  • 'The Orphan' (1885) - Depicts a solitary child, abandoned and vulnerable, gazing into the void with eyes filled with longing and sorrow, embodying the plight of the forgotten and neglected.
  • 'Anguish' (1878) - Portrays a tormented soul, consumed by inner turmoil, their face contorted in pain as they grapple with the demons of their existence.
  • 'Souvenir of Auvern' (1873) - Captures the tranquility of a serene countryside landscape, with rolling hills and picturesque villages bathed in the warm glow of the setting sun.
  • 'Barn Scene with Sheep' (19th century) - Evokes the rustic charm of rural life, with a quaint barn nestled amidst lush green pastures, where sheep graze contentedly under the watchful eye of the farmer.
  • 'Flock of Sheep in the Snow' (19th century) - Conjures a scene of winter wonderland, as a flock of sheep traverse a snow-covered landscape, their woolly coats standing out against the pristine white backdrop.
  • 'Sheep and a Herder in a Winter Landscape' (19th century) - Depicts a harmonious bond between man and animal, as a herder tends to his flock amidst the tranquil beauty of a snowy landscape, fostering a sense of peace and unity.
  • 'Flock of Sheep Stranded in a Blizzard' (19th century) - Conveys the harsh realities of nature's wrath, as a flock of sheep battles against the fierce winds and biting cold of a blizzard, highlighting the resilience and determination of both man and beast in the face of adversity.
